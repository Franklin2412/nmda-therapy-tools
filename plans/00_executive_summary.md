## üéØ What We're Proposing

Development of **six free, web-based applications** designed to support children recovering from autoimmune NMDA encephalitis in the following areas:

1. **Memory & Cognitive Function** (Memory Garden)
2. **Speech & Language** (SpeakEasy Studio)
3. **Attention & Executive Function** (Think Tank Challenge)

## üí° Why This Matters

### The Need
Children with NMDA encephalitis often require **intensive, prolonged rehabilitation** (3+ hours daily, 6 days/week). Between therapy sessions, families need:
- Engaging practice activities
- Progress tracking tools
- Age-appropriate skill reinforcement
- Ways to actively participate in recovery

### Current Gap
- Most rehabilitation apps are **generic** (not designed for encephalitis)
- Commercial tools are often **expensive**
- Few tools specifically address the **cognitive-behavioral-speech profile** of NMDA encephalitis
- Limited **child-friendly, gamified** options

### Our Solution
- **Free, open-source** web applications
- **Evidence-based** (grounded in encephalitis rehabilitation research)
- **Clinically-guided** (seeking your expertise)
- **Accessible** (works on any device with a browser)
- **Privacy-focused** (all data stays on user's device)

## üìä Clinical Research Foundation

Our proposals are based on peer-reviewed research showing:

### Cognitive Rehabilitation Needs
- **36-70% of children** experience ongoing academic/cognitive difficulties
- Key deficits: **attention, memory (especially working/verbal), executive function**
- **Early, intensive intervention** improves outcomes
- **Multidisciplinary approach** is essential

### Speech & Language Needs
- **Mutism, echolalia, reduced speech** are common symptoms
- **Language deficits** contribute significantly to overall disability
- **Speech therapy** is core component of recovery
- **Interactive, repetitive practice** supports neural rewiring

### Behavioral & Emotional Needs
- **Behavioral changes** (agitation, impulsivity, anxiety) are prevalent
- **Antecedent behavior management** and **supportive interventions** are preferred
- **Emotional regulation skills** must be relearned
- **Social skills practice** aids reintegration

### Digital Tool Effectiveness
- **Virtual reality and digital platforms** show promise in pediatric brain injury rehab
- **Gamification increases engagement** and practice time
- **Progress tracking** enables data-driven therapy adjustments
- **Accessibility** makes therapy more flexible for families

**Sources:** NIH research, Neurology journals, Encephalitis Society clinical guidelines, Children's Hospital programs (citations in detailed documents)

## üèóÔ∏è Proposed Applications (Brief Overview)

### 1. Memory Garden üå±
**Targets:** Working memory, visual memory, sequential processing, attention

**Activities:**
- Flower Memory Match (classic matching game)
- Garden Sequence (remember and recreate bloom patterns)
- Treasure Hunt (spatial memory)
- Story Seeds (narrative recall)

**Why:** Memory deficits are among the most persistent challenges in NMDA encephalitis recovery.

---

### 2. SpeakEasy Studio üé§
**Targets:** Articulation, sentence formation, vocabulary, listening comprehension

**Activities:**
- Sound Safari (phonics practice with voice recording)
- Sentence Builder (drag-and-drop grammar)
- Story Teller (narrative practice with prompts)
- Word Hunt (vocabulary building)
- Listen & Do (following instructions)

**Why:** Speech dysfunction (mutism, echolalia) is a hallmark symptom requiring sustained practice.

---

### 3. Think Tank Challenge üß†
**Targets:** Attention, problem-solving, executive function, processing speed

**Activities:**
- Pattern Detective (pattern recognition)
- Sort & Stack (categorization, planning)
- Focus Flash ("I Spy," attention training)
- Puzzle Path (maze navigation, planning)
- Number Ninjas (math-based cognitive flex)

**Why:** Executive function and attention deficits significantly impact academic reintegration.

---

### 4. Emotion Explorer üòä
**Targets:** Emotion recognition, regulation, social awareness, coping skills

**Activities:**
- Feelings Faces (emotion identification)
- Situation Stories (social scenarios with choices)
- Calm Corner (breathing exercises, mindfulness)
- Emotion Diary (mood tracking)
- Social Detective (body language, social cues)

**Why:** Behavioral changes and emotional dysregulation require explicit teaching of coping strategies.

---

### 5. Daily Skills Companion üìÖ
**Targets:** Task sequencing, time management, organization, independence

**Activities:**
- Visual Schedule Builder (pictorial routines)
- Task Breakdown (step-by-step checklists)
- Routine Reminders (gentle prompts)
- Achievement Board (progress visualization)

**Why:** Rebuilding daily living skills supports functional independence.

---

### 6. Movement & Coordination Corner üèÉ
**Targets:** Motor planning, bilateral coordination, following instructions, body awareness

**Activities:**
- Simon Says Digital (movement sequences)
- Dance Party (rhythm and coordination)
- Yoga for Kids (balance, body awareness)
- Movement Memory (physical + cognitive)

**Why:** Movement disorders are common; physical activity supports overall neurological recovery.
